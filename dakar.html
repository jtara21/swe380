Dakar, a city to visit!

Senegal's capital, Dakar, is often used as a base for touring the country but is well worth a trip on its own. Each of its districts is different and can be explored with pleasure on foot or by taxi.Note that Dakar is located at the westernmost point of the African continent, on the peninsula of Cape Verde. Therefore there are many things to do and visit in the city.

Where to stay?

There are many good hotels in Dakar such as Terrou bi, Radisson, Pullman, Novotel, Residences Mamoune... It is also possible to find places to stay using Airbnb. There are also appartments avaible for tourists. We are here to help you choose the option thats suits you best.

Places to visit

You can find many places to visit in Dakar. Here are the most popular places to visit:

  • The African Renaissance Monument: it is a 52 m (171 ft) tall bronze statue located on top of one of the twin hills known as Collines des Mamelles.
  • Goree Island: it was from the 15th to the 19th century the largest slave trade center on the African coast. This "island of memory" is for the universal conscience the symbol of the slave trade with its procession of suffering, tears and death. There is also Ngor Island, a really nice place to relax
  • The Pink Lake: Lake Retba lies north of the Cap Vert peninsula in Senegal. It is named for its pink waters caused by Dunaliella salina algae and is known for its high salt content.
  • Mosques or the Cathedral of Dakar if you are religious. Religion has an important place in the country so you can find beautiful mosques and a really nice Cathedral
  • The Museum of Black Civilisations: it is a state museum that was designed to highlight the contribution of Africa to the cultural and scientific heritage of the world.

Activities

There many fun activities to do in Dakar like paintball, cinema, quad, virtual reality game and laser game at sea plaza mall, jet ski and surf... There are also really nice restaurants at Corniche des Almadies(most of them are seaside), Maison Kayser, Kiosk or Melo patisserie for brunch a melo...
